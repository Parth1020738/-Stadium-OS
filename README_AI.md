# Aegis Smart Stadium OS - AI Operations Center & Decision Support Engine

This document outlines the architecture, components, and integration parameters of the Phase 10D AI Operations Center and Decision Support Engine in the Aegis Smart Stadium OS.

## Overview

The AI Operations Center serves as the central intelligence node of the stadium operating system. It continuously aggregates live events, metrics, materialized views, and logs from every subsystem, correlates indicators, and generates actionable, explainable recommendations for human operators without executing critical operations autonomously.

## Key Services

1. **AI Decision Service (`ai_decision_service.py`)**: Gathers live crowd density, transit statuses, incident counts, and system metrics to produce structured recommendations, confidence parameters, and audit entries.
2. **Risk Prediction Service (`RiskPredictionService`)**: Calculates real-time risks across seven vectors (Crowd, Medical, Security, Fire, Transit, Accessibility, and Overall Stadium Risk).
3. **AI Copilot Service (`copilot_service.py`)**: Evaluates natural-language queries from operators, matching them against standard operating procedures (SOPs) and historical incidents using a RAG-based logic engine.
4. **Knowledge Integration**: Integrates directly with the Knowledge Service for playbooks, SOPs, and citations.

## Data Schema & Persistence

All records are written to a resilient SQLite/PostgreSQL schema:
- `AIRecommendation`: Recommendations generated by the engine.
- `AIRiskAssessment`: Live risk score logs.
- `AIExplanation`: RAG citations and rationale.
- `AIDecision`: Operator accept/reject actions.
- `AITimeline`: Stream of decision events.
- `AIFeedback`: Operator quality ratings.

## Integration Channels

- **Kafka**: Events published to `ai.recommendation.created`, `ai.recommendation.accepted`, `ai.recommendation.rejected`, `ai.risk.updated`, and `ai.timeline.created`.
- **Redis**: Caching layer for active risk scores and recommendation summaries with built-in fallback to SQL databases.
